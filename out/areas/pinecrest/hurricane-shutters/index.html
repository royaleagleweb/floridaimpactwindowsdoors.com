Hurricane Shutter Types Available in Pinecrest

We install every major type of hurricane shutter approved for Miami-Dade County. Choose the style that fits your Pinecrest home's architecture, your lifestyle, and your budget.

Accordion Shutters

Permanently mounted folding shutters that slide along a top and bottom track. Fast deployment and reliable protection for any opening size.

Roll-Down Shutters

Motorized or manual roll-down shutters housed in a compact overhead box. The ultimate in convenience with one-touch storm protection.

Colonial Shutters

Decorative louvered shutters that flank your windows and latch closed for storms. Classic Southern charm meets hurricane-grade strength.

Bahama Shutters

Top-hinged tropical-style shutters that prop open for shade and lower to lock position for hurricane protection.

Storm Panel Shutters

Removable aluminum or clear polycarbonate panels that bolt into pre-installed tracks. An affordable and proven protection option.

Clear Storm Panels

Transparent polycarbonate panels that let natural light in while providing tested hurricane protection. See outside during the storm.

Hurricane Shutters FAQ for Pinecrest

How much do hurricane shutters cost in Pinecrest?
Hurricane shutter costs in Pinecrest depend on the type and number of openings. Storm panels are the most affordable option, starting around $7 to $15 per square foot. Accordion shutters typically range from $15 to $30 per square foot, while motorized roll-down shutters range from $25 to $60 per square foot. Florida Impact Windows & Doors provides free in-home estimates in Pinecrest so you can compare options and find the best solution for your budget.
Which hurricane shutters are best for Pinecrest homes?
The ideal shutter type for your Pinecrest home depends on your priorities. Roll-down shutters offer the most convenience, especially for multi-story homes or if you travel frequently. Accordion shutters provide excellent value with easy manual operation. Colonial and Bahama shutters add daily curb appeal to Pinecrest homes while providing on-demand storm protection. Our experts will recommend the best option during your free consultation based on your home layout and Miami-Dade County requirements.
Do hurricane shutters meet Miami-Dade County building codes?
Yes, all hurricane shutters we install in Pinecrest are tested and approved to meet Florida Building Code requirements, including the strict High Velocity Hurricane Zone standards enforced in Miami-Dade County. Every product carries a Florida Product Approval number, and Florida Impact Windows & Doors handles all required Miami-Dade County permits and inspections for your installation.
Can I install hurricane shutters on a home that already has impact windows in Pinecrest?
While most Pinecrest homes choose one system or the other, you can add hurricane shutters over non-impact windows and standard doors while keeping impact products on other openings. This hybrid approach is common in Pinecrest when homeowners want full protection but have budget constraints.
