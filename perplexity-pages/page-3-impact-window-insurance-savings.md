# How Impact Windows Lower Insurance Premiums in Florida (2026 Guide)

## Overview

Florida homeowners pay some of the highest property insurance premiums in the nation, largely due to hurricane risk. Installing impact windows is one of the most effective ways to reduce those costs — Florida law actually **requires insurers to offer discounts** for hurricane mitigation features.

## How Much Can You Save?

Homeowners with impact windows typically save **$500 to $2,000+ per year** on hurricane insurance premiums. The exact discount depends on:

- Your insurance carrier and policy
- The scope of protection (all openings vs. partial)
- Whether you have other mitigation features (roof, secondary water barrier)
- Your home's location and wind zone

The **maximum savings** come from protecting **all openings** (windows and doors) with impact-rated products. Combined with a code-compliant roof, homeowners can see premium reductions of **20% to 45%** ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/faq/)).

## The Wind Mitigation Inspection

After installing impact windows, you need a **Wind Mitigation Inspection** — a standardized assessment performed by a licensed inspector. This report documents:

1. **Roof covering** — Age and type of roof material
2. **Roof-to-wall connections** — How the roof attaches to walls (clips, straps, etc.)
3. **Roof deck attachment** — Method of securing roof sheathing
4. **Roof geometry** — Hip vs. gable roof shape
5. **Secondary water resistance** — Barrier under roof covering
6. **Opening protection** — This is where impact windows are documented

The inspector verifies that all openings meet Florida Building Code standards. Impact windows with a valid Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA) or Florida Product Approval receive the highest rating.

### What the Inspection Costs

A Wind Mitigation Inspection typically costs **$75 to $150** — a minimal expense compared to the annual savings it unlocks. Many impact window installers can recommend qualified inspectors ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/services/impact-windows/)).

## Understanding Florida's Mitigation Discount Law

Florida Statute 627.0629 requires all property insurers to provide premium discounts or credits for hurricane mitigation improvements. This isn't optional — insurers **must** reduce your premiums when you provide a valid Wind Mitigation Inspection showing approved protection.

### Discount Categories for Opening Protection

| Protection Level | Typical Discount |
|---|---|
| No protection | 0% |
| Basic shutters (partial openings) | 5–15% |
| All openings protected (shutters) | 15–25% |
| All openings with impact-rated products | 20–45%* |

*Maximum discounts achieved when combined with other mitigation features like hip roof and roof-to-wall straps.
