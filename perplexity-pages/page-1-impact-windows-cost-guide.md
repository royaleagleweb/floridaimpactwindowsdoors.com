# How Much Do Impact Windows Cost in South Florida? (2026 Price Guide)

## Overview

Impact windows are a critical investment for South Florida homeowners living in hurricane-prone areas. Understanding the costs involved helps homeowners budget effectively and choose the right products for their homes.

## Average Cost Breakdown

The cost of impact windows in South Florida typically ranges from **$40 to $100+ per square foot**, fully installed. For a standard-sized window, homeowners can expect to pay between **$500 and $1,500 per window**, depending on several key factors.

For a full-home replacement on an average South Florida residence with 15 to 20 windows, the total generally falls in the **$10,000 to $30,000 range**, though larger homes with custom requirements can exceed that significantly.

### Factors That Affect Pricing

- **Window size and style**: Standard single-hung windows cost less than large sliding glass or custom-shaped windows
- **Frame material**: Vinyl frames are typically more affordable than aluminum; wood-clad frames are the most expensive
- **Glass type**: Standard laminated glass costs less than Low-E coated, insulated, or tinted options
- **Design Pressure (DP) rating**: Higher wind resistance ratings (required in Miami-Dade and Broward's HVHZ) cost more
- **Custom shapes**: Arched, circular, or non-standard openings require custom fabrication
- **Labor rates**: Miami-Dade and Broward counties tend to have slightly higher installation costs due to stricter building code requirements

## Return on Investment

Impact windows deliver substantial financial returns beyond storm protection:

- **Insurance savings**: Florida law requires insurers to offer discounts for hurricane mitigation features. Homeowners typically save **$500 to $2,000+ annually** on premiums. Combined with a code-compliant roof, discounts can reach 20–45% ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/services/impact-windows/))
- **Home value increase**: Impact windows can increase resale value by **$10,000 to $30,000** or more. In South Florida, buyers actively seek homes with impact protection ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/faq/))
- **Energy savings**: Low-E coated impact windows reduce solar heat gain by up to 70%, significantly cutting cooling costs ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/services/energy-efficient-windows/))
- **No shutters needed**: Impact windows eliminate the ongoing cost and hassle of purchasing, storing, and installing hurricane shutters

## Financing Options

Several financing paths make impact windows accessible:

- **Traditional financing**: 12 to 120 month terms with competitive rates and promotional zero-percent interest periods for qualified buyers
- **PACE program** (Property Assessed Clean Energy): Finances improvements through property tax bills with no money down and no minimum credit score. Available throughout Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/financing/))
