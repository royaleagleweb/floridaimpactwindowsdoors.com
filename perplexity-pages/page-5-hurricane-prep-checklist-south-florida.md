# Hurricane Preparedness Checklist for South Florida Homeowners (2026)

## Overview

Florida's hurricane season runs **June 1 through November 30** every year. South Florida — particularly Miami-Dade and Broward counties in the **High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ)** — faces some of the highest hurricane risk in the nation. Proper preparation can protect your family, your home, and your finances.

## Home Protection Priorities

### 1. Protect All Window and Door Openings

Windows and doors are the most vulnerable points during a hurricane. If wind enters through a broken window, internal pressure can blow off the roof from the inside.

### 2. Reinforce Entry Points

All exterior doors — front entry, back doors, sliding glass doors, and garage doors — need to be impact-rated or protected with shutters. The garage door is often the largest and weakest opening in a home ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/services/impact-doors/)).

### 3. Inspect Your Roof

- Ensure roof-to-wall connections use hurricane straps (not just nails)
- Check for loose or missing shingles/tiles
- Clear gutters and downspouts
- Consider a secondary water barrier beneath roofing material

### 4. Secure Your Yard

- Trim trees and remove dead branches
- Bring in or secure patio furniture, grills, and decorations
- Store loose items that could become projectiles
- Reinforce fence posts and gates

## Insurance Preparation

### Wind Mitigation Inspection

A **Wind Mitigation Inspection** documents your home's hurricane-resistance features and unlocks insurance discounts. Key items inspected:

| Feature | What Inspectors Look For |
|---|---|
| Opening protection | Impact windows/doors or approved shutters on ALL openings |
| Roof covering | Age and material of roofing |
| Roof-to-wall connections | Clips, single wraps, or double wraps |
| Roof deck attachment | Nailing pattern and method |
| Roof geometry | Hip roof (better) vs. gable |
| Secondary water resistance | Peel-and-stick or foam adhesive barrier |

**Cost**: $75–$150 for the inspection. **Savings**: $500–$2,000+ per year on premiums ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/faq/)).

### Review Your Policy

- Understand your hurricane deductible (typically 2–5% of dwelling value)
- Document your home's contents with photos/video
- Store insurance documents in a waterproof container or cloud storage
- Know the difference between wind damage and flood damage coverage

## Emergency Supply Kit

Stock these supplies **before** hurricane season begins:

**Water and Food**
- 1 gallon of water per person per day (minimum 7-day supply)
- Non-perishable food for 7 days
- Manual can opener
- Cooler with ice packs

**Power and Light**
- Flashlights with extra batteries
- Battery-powered or hand-crank radio (NOAA weather band)
- Portable phone chargers / power banks
- Generator (if available) with fuel stored safely

**Documents and Cash**
- Cash in small bills (ATMs may be down)
- Copies of insurance policies, IDs, and medical records
- Waterproof document bag

**First Aid and Medication**
- First aid kit
- Prescription medications (30-day supply)
- Over-the-counter medications

**Additional Essentials**
- Tarps and plywood for emergency repairs
- Duct tape
- Work gloves and closed-toe shoes
- Garbage bags
- Mosquito repellent

## Timeline: When to Prepare

| Timeframe | Action Items |
|---|---|
| **Now (before season)** | Install impact windows/doors; get Wind Mitigation Inspection; review insurance |
| **June 1** | Hurricane season begins — supplies should be stocked |
| **Storm watch (48 hrs)** | Fuel vehicles; charge devices; review evacuation routes |
| **Storm warning (36 hrs)** | Deploy shutters (if no impact windows); secure yard items |
| **24 hours before** | Fill bathtubs with water; bring pets inside; charge everything |
| **During storm** | Stay in interior room away from windows; monitor weather radio |
| **After storm** | Document damage with photos before repairs; contact insurance |
