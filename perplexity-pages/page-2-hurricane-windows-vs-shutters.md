# Hurricane Impact Windows vs. Shutters: Which Is Better for South Florida Homes?

## Overview

South Florida homeowners in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) are required by Florida Building Code to have approved hurricane protection on all openings. The two main options are impact windows and hurricane shutters. Each has distinct advantages depending on budget, lifestyle, and long-term goals.

## Impact Windows: Pros and Cons

### How They Work

Hurricane impact windows use **laminated glass construction** — two panes of tempered glass bonded with a polyvinyl butyral (PVB) or ethylene-vinyl acetate (EVA) interlayer. If debris strikes the glass, the interlayer holds broken pieces in place, preventing wind and water intrusion ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/services/impact-windows/)).

Products installed in Miami-Dade and Broward counties must pass the **Miami-Dade large missile impact test**: a 9-pound 2×4 lumber fired at 34 mph, followed by pressure cycling that simulates sustained hurricane-force winds.

### Advantages

- **24/7 protection** — No preparation needed when a storm approaches
- **Insurance discounts** — Savings of 20–45% on hurricane premiums when combined with a code-compliant roof ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/faq/))
- **UV protection** — Blocks 99% of UV rays, protecting furniture and flooring
- **Noise reduction** — Reduces outside noise by up to 60%
- **Security** — Laminated glass resists break-ins; repeated blows crack but don't penetrate
- **Home value** — Increases resale value by $10,000–$30,000+ ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/faq/))
- **Energy efficiency** — Low-E options reduce cooling costs significantly
- **No storage needed** — Eliminates the need to store panels or shutters

### Disadvantages

- Higher upfront cost ($10,000–$30,000 for a full home)
- Longer lead time for manufacturing (4–8 weeks)
- Cracked glass requires full panel replacement

### Advantages

- **Lower upfront cost** — Storm panels start at under $10 per square foot
- **Code compliant** — All types meet Florida Building Code requirements
- **Quick installation** — Shutters can be installed in days, not weeks

### Disadvantages

- **Require deployment** — Must be closed before every storm (except permanent styles)
- **Storage** — Removable panels need a garage or storage area
- **Darkness** — Block all natural light when closed
- **No daily benefits** — Don't provide UV, noise, or security protection year-round
- **Maintenance** — Tracks and mechanisms need regular cleaning and lubrication
- **Aesthetics** — Some types (accordion, panels) are not visually appealing

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose impact windows if:**
- You plan to stay in your home long-term (ROI improves over time)
- You want daily benefits beyond storm protection
- You're doing a renovation or new construction
- You want maximum insurance savings
- You don't want the hassle of deploying shutters before each storm

**Choose hurricane shutters if:**
- You're on a tight budget
- You need temporary protection for a rental or investment property
- You plan to upgrade to impact windows later

**Many homeowners use a hybrid approach** — impact windows on main living areas and shutters on less-used spaces like garages or utility rooms.
