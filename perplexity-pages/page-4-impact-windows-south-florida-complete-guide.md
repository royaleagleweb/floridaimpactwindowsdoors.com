# Complete Guide to Impact Windows in South Florida: Everything Homeowners Need to Know

## What Are Hurricane Impact Windows?

Hurricane impact windows feature **laminated glass construction** — two panes of tempered glass bonded with a polyvinyl butyral (PVB) or ethylene-vinyl acetate (EVA) interlayer. If the glass cracks from flying debris during a hurricane, the interlayer holds the broken pieces in place, preventing dangerous wind and water from entering the home ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/services/impact-windows/)).

In South Florida's **High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ)** — which covers Miami-Dade and Broward counties — products must pass the rigorous **Miami-Dade large missile impact test**: a 9-pound 2×4 lumber piece fired at 34 mph, followed by thousands of pressure cycles simulating sustained hurricane winds.

## Why South Florida Homes Need Impact Windows

South Florida sits in one of the most hurricane-vulnerable regions in the United States. The area faces unique challenges:

- **Hurricane season** runs June 1 through November 30 every year
- **Miami-Dade and Broward counties** are classified as HVHZ, requiring the strictest building standards in Florida
- **Florida Building Code** requires impact protection for new construction and major renovations
- Older homes built before current codes remain especially vulnerable to storm damage

### By Frame Material

- **Aluminum**: Most popular in South Florida. Durable, corrosion-resistant (with proper coating), slim profiles for maximum glass area. Meets HVHZ structural requirements easily.
- **Vinyl**: Energy efficient, low maintenance, never needs painting. Excellent for coastal environments. Typically more affordable than aluminum.
- **Wood-Clad**: Premium option offering traditional aesthetics with impact protection. Popular for historic homes and upscale renovations.

### By Glass Performance

- **Standard laminated**: Meets impact requirements; basic UV and noise protection
- **Low-E coated**: Reflects solar heat; reduces cooling costs by up to 70% ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/services/energy-efficient-windows/))
- **Insulated (IGU)**: Double-pane with air or argon gap; maximum energy efficiency
- **Tinted**: Reduces glare and solar heat gain; available in bronze, gray, and green

## Design Pressure (DP) Ratings Explained

Impact windows are rated by **Design Pressure (DP)**, which measures resistance to wind load:

- **DP 35–45**: Suitable for inland areas with lower wind exposure
- **DP 50–60**: Standard for most South Florida residential applications
- **DP 65+**: Required for high-rise buildings, direct waterfront, and high-exposure locations
- **HVHZ-rated**: Products with a Miami-Dade **Notice of Acceptance (NOA)** — required for Miami-Dade and Broward counties

Higher DP ratings mean the window can withstand greater wind speeds and pressure differentials during a hurricane.

## How Long Do Impact Windows Last?

High-quality impact windows last **25 to 30 years or more**. The laminated glass does not degrade over time. Modern aluminum and vinyl frames are engineered to resist salt air corrosion — critical in South Florida's coastal environment ([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/faq/)).

### Maintenance Requirements

Maintaining impact windows is straightforward:

- **Monthly**: Clean glass with mild soap or glass cleaner and soft cloth
- **Quarterly**: Vacuum tracks to remove sand and debris
- **Twice yearly**: Apply silicone lubricant to hardware and moving parts
- **Annually**: Inspect weatherstripping, locks, and glass seals

Avoid abrasive cleaners or razor blades, which can damage the glass surface.

## Can Impact Windows Be Installed in Older Homes?

Yes. Many pre-1990s South Florida homes have non-impact windows that can be upgraded. Considerations for older homes include:

- **Non-standard sizes**: Older openings may require custom fabrication
- **Structural reinforcement**: Heavier impact assemblies may need additional support
- **Historic districts**: Modern impact windows come in styles that complement traditional architecture
- **Stucco and masonry**: Experienced installers know how to work with older construction methods

([Source](https://floridaimpactwindowsdoors.com/faq/))
